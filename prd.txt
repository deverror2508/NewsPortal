1. Executive Summary
This Product Requirement Document (PRD) outlines the complete specifications for building a full-featured News Portal Web Application. The platform will serve as a modern, scalable digital news ecosystem enabling journalists, authors, and registered users to create, publish, and consume news content across multiple categories.
The News Portal will be built on a proven MERN-adjacent stack: React.js on the frontend, Node.js + Express.js on the backend, and MongoDB as the primary datastore. The platform prioritizes performance, SEO discoverability, role-based access control, and mobile-first responsive design.

Core Value Proposition
A single, unified platform where readers discover categorized news, authors publish rich-media articles, and administrators govern content quality — all through an intuitive, fast-loading, SEO-optimized interface.

2. Problem Statement
The digital news landscape is fragmented. Readers struggle to find credible, categorized, and searchable content in one place. Independent journalists and content creators lack affordable, easy-to-use platforms to publish news without depending on large media conglomerates. Additionally, most lightweight news platforms sacrifice either SEO performance, editorial control, or user experience.

Key Pain Points
Readers cannot efficiently search, filter, or discover relevant news by category, date, or keyword on smaller platforms.
Independent authors lack a self-service publishing tool with rich-text editing and image upload capabilities.
News aggregators offer no editorial oversight, leading to misinformation and low-quality submissions.
Most news portals are not mobile-optimized, alienating smartphone-first audiences.
Small news websites are poorly indexed by search engines due to missing SEO metadata and non-semantic URLs.

3. Objectives & Goals
Primary Objectives
Build a production-grade news portal with robust user authentication and role-based permissions.
Enable rich-content article creation with image upload, formatting tools, and category tagging.
Implement powerful search and multi-dimensional filtering (keyword, date, category).
Establish an admin-governed approval workflow for user-submitted articles.
Deliver a mobile-first, accessible, and SEO-optimized frontend.

Business Goals
Achieve a Page Speed Insights score of 85+ on mobile within 3 months of launch.
Onboard 500 registered users and 50 active authors in the first 6 months.
Maintain 99.5% uptime with a mean time to recovery (MTTR) under 30 minutes.
Target top-5 search ranking for primary news category keywords within 12 months.

7. Functional Requirements
7.1 Authentication Module
FR-001: System shall support email/password registration with bcrypt hashing (minimum 12 salt rounds).
FR-002: System shall issue JWT access tokens (15-min expiry) and refresh tokens (7-day expiry) on successful login.
FR-003: System shall validate email uniqueness at registration time.
FR-004: System shall enforce password policy: minimum 8 characters, at least one uppercase, one number.
FR-005: System shall blacklist refresh tokens on logout.

7.2 Article Module
FR-006: System shall support a WYSIWYG rich-text editor (Quill.js or TipTap) with bold, italic, headings, links, and lists.
FR-007: System shall accept image uploads up to 5 MB in JPEG, PNG, and WebP formats.
FR-008: System shall auto-generate SEO-friendly slugs from article titles (e.g., 'My Great Article' → 'my-great-article').
FR-009: System shall support draft, pending, published, and rejected article statuses.
FR-010: System shall record createdAt and updatedAt timestamps for every article.

7.3 Search & Filter Module
FR-011: System shall implement full-text search on article title and body using MongoDB Atlas Search or a text index.
FR-012: System shall support compound filtering: category + date range + keyword simultaneously.
FR-013: System shall paginate results with configurable page size (default 10, max 50).
FR-014: System shall return search results within 500ms for indexed queries.

7.4 Category Module
FR-015: System shall enforce unique category slugs.
FR-016: System shall prevent deletion of a category that has articles assigned to it (return 409 Conflict).
FR-017: System shall display article counts per category in the admin panel.

7.5 Approval Workflow
FR-018: Submitted articles shall enter 'pending' status automatically.
FR-019: Admin approval shall trigger a status change to 'published' and make the article publicly visible.
FR-020: Admin rejection shall store a rejection reason string and notify the author (in-app notification, Phase 2 email).
FR-021: Authors may resubmit a rejected article after editing.

9. System Architecture (High-Level)
The application follows a three-tier architecture with a decoupled frontend SPA, a RESTful API backend, and a document database. A CDN layer caches static assets and images globally.

Architecture Pattern
Client-Server (SPA + REST API) with a CDN edge layer. The backend is stateless, enabling horizontal scaling via container orchestration (Docker + optional Kubernetes in production).

9.2 Data Flow — Article Submission
Author creates/edits article in React rich-text editor and clicks Submit.
React frontend POSTs to POST /api/articles with JWT Authorization header.
Express middleware validates JWT, checks Author role, sanitizes body.
Article document is saved to MongoDB with status: 'pending'.
Admin dashboard polls/receives notification of new pending article.
Admin approves → PATCH /api/articles/:id/approve → status: 'published'.
Article becomes publicly accessible via GET /api/articles/:slug.

Progressive Disclosure: Complex admin features are hidden from readers via role-gated routes.
Consistency: Shared component library (buttons, inputs, cards) ensures visual cohesion.
Speed Perception: Skeleton loaders replace spinners for perceived faster loading.

14. Future Scope
Phase 2 (Months 4–8)
Comments System: Threaded comments with author moderation and admin oversight.
Email Notifications: SendGrid-powered emails for approval outcomes, comment replies.
Dark Mode: User-toggled dark/light theme persisted in localStorage.
Social Authentication: OAuth 2.0 login via Google and GitHub.
Bookmark / Reading List: Registered users save articles for later reading.

Phase 3 (Months 9–14)
Analytics Dashboard: Admin-facing charts for article views, user growth, and category trends using Chart.js or Recharts.
Newsletter Integration: Email digest of top articles via Mailchimp/SendGrid.
Progressive Web App (PWA): Offline reading support and home screen installation.
Multi-language Support (i18n): Hindi and regional language article support.
Monetization: Ad slot management and sponsored article tagging.

Phase 4 (Months 15+)
Recommendation Engine: ML-based 'You May Also Like' personalization using collaborative filtering.
Mobile Apps: React Native iOS and Android applications sharing the same API.
Federated Search: Integration with external news APIs (NewsAPI, RSS feeds) for aggregated content.
Video Article Support: Embedded video with thumbnail and transcript generation.

15. Assumptions & Risks
15.1 Assumptions
The application will be deployed to a cloud provider (AWS, GCP, or Azure) with managed MongoDB Atlas.
Image storage will use Cloudinary free tier initially, upgrading on volume growth.
All admin users will be manually seeded during initial deployment.
English will be the sole supported language for Phase 1.
